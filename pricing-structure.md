# Pricing Structure

## Client-Facing Pricing

| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Upfront setup fee (per shop) | $5,000 |
| Monthly retainer | $2,500–$3,000/month |
| First-month total (setup + first month) | ~$6,000 |

> Example close from the video: *"Six grand for the first month and then three grand every month after that."*

---

## The 7x Return Math (Value Justification)

This is the core argument used to close clients. The numbers are gathered on the discovery call and then used to show ROI.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average revenue per customer visit | $2,000 |
| Customers recovered per month (conservative) | ~10 |
| Monthly revenue recovered for the shop | ~$20,000 |
| Monthly cost to the shop | $3,000 |
| Return on investment | **~7x** |

The pitch: *"I'm bringing them back $20,000 every single month simply by having a better texting flow — and they pay me $3,000 a month for it."*

---

## What's Being Sold

A niche software solution (example: **Vroom** — AI-powered follow-up texts for auto shops) that:

- Replaces generic, robotic automated texts with personalised AI conversations
- Recovers declined repair work and lapsed customers
- Requires no ongoing manual work from the shop owner

---

## Pricing Philosophy

- **Outcome-based pricing**: clients pay because the solution generates measurable, quantifiable revenue
- **The discovery call is the sales tool**: gather the shop's own numbers (lost revenue, average ticket, churn), then close them using those numbers in the pitch deck
- **Start with an MVP**: charge for results, not polish — refine the product after clients are paying
